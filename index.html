Mathematical Perspectives: Viewing 3I/ATLAS Through Different Coordinate Systems

Every astronomical observation depends on the coordinate system we choose. Let's explore how 3I/ATLAS appears from different mathematical perspectives and reference frames.

🌞 Heliocentric Coordinates

Sun-centered perspective

r = a(1-e²)/(1+e cos θ)

Origin: Center of the Sun

Perspective: 3I/ATLAS follows a hyperbolic trajectory (e > 1)

Perihelion: ~11 AU from Sun (October 30, 2025)

Eccentricity: e ≈ 1.03 (hyperbolic orbit)

🌌 Galactic Coordinates

Galaxy-centered perspective

l = arctan(y/x), b = arcsin(z/r)

Origin: Galactic center

Perspective: 3I/ATLAS as a traveler between star systems

Galactic Motion: ~220 km/s orbital velocity around galaxy

Interstellar Speed: ~137,000 mph relative to Sun

⚖️ Barycentric Coordinates

Solar system center-of-mass perspective

F = GMm/r² (gravitational influence)

Origin: Solar system barycenter

Perspective: Accounting for all planetary masses

Jupiter Effect: Largest gravitational perturbation

Path Deviation: Minimal due to high velocity

⚡ Relativistic Perspective

Einstein's spacetime curvature

ds² = c²dt² - dx² - dy² - dz²

Time Dilation: Negligible at 0.00046c

Spacetime Curvature: Follows geodesics in curved spacetime

Frame Dragging: Minimal effect from Sun's rotation
